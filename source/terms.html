  2. CFD

    CFD stands for a “Contract for Difference”, which means that it is not the underlying asset that you buy but rather a contract to settle the difference between the opening and closing price of the asset. CFD trade is usually a margin trade, which means that you only have to pay a small share of your position’s total cost. This is generally known as “Leverage,” and even insignificant market fluctuations can have a major impact on your trading account – either negative or positive.

    If the market movement is against you, your total loss may exceed the funds invested in a particular position. You are responsible for all losses in your account, up to and including all the funds in your account.

    CFDs are not suitable for the long-term investor. If you hold CFDs open over a long period of time, the associated costs, such as overnight charges, grow and buying the underlying asset may turn out to be more profitable.
